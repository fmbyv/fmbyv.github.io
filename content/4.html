Scrum Anti-patterns


Scrum and Kanban are two popular frameworks used to help teams and organizations become more agile. However, while both are effective tools, there are certain anti-patterns that can quickly render them useless.

In this blog post, I discuss the pros and cons of both scrum and kanban, the differences between them, and how to choose the right framework for your team.

Scrum is a well-known and popular framework for managing projects. It focuses on iterative development, with small teams working in sprints to deliver a product. It is highly structured, with roles, meetings, and artifacts that must be respected. However, it is sometimes seen as overly rigid, and teams may struggle to remain agile with so many rules in place.

Kanban, on the other hand, is a less structured but still effective framework. It is based on the principle of continuous improvement, with teams taking a more evolutionary approach to development. There are no sprints, and tasks are organized into workflows that help teams focus on one task at a time. The lack of structure can sometimes lead to teams feeling overwhelmed, and the lack of progress tracking can make it difficult to determine whether the team is going in the right direction or not.

When choosing a framework for your team, it is important to consider the strengths and weaknesses of each. Scrum is great for teams that need structure and clear deadlines, while Kanban is better for teams that need flexibility and continuous improvement.

Ultimately, the best framework for your team will depend on your team's goals, skills, and preferences. It's important to remember that there is no one-size-fits-all solution, and that the best framework for your team may not be the same as the best framework for another team.

By understanding the pros and cons of both scrum and kanban, you can make an informed decision about which framework is best for your team.
